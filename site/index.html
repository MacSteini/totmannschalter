A fully self-hosted dead man's switch for email

Confirms presence. Escalates only when confirmation stops.

totmann sends regular check-in emails to you. As long as you keep confirming that you are still there, nothing else happens. If those confirmations stop for long enough, it contacts the people you chose in advance.

Key features

What totmann handles, and what stays in your control.

Fully self-hosted

Runs on infrastructure you control, without a third-party service sitting in the middle.

You choose the rhythm

Set the check-in schedule that fits your life, instead of adapting yourself to a fixed cadence.

Intentional confirmation

Opening an email is not enough on its own. You make a deliberate confirmation, which helps prevent accidental resets.

Protected links

Confirmation and recipient links are protected so unsuitable or accidental requests do not trigger the real action.

Grace before escalation

The system waits through your configured window and safety buffer before it treats silence as a real problem.

Predefined contact list

The contact scope is fixed in advance, so the system does not improvise when it matters.

Recipient-specific messages

Different recipients can receive the message content you prepared for them, instead of one shared generic note.

Receipt confirmation

A recipient can confirm receipt so the same event does not keep generating unnecessary follow-up.

Private file delivery

Optional private files can be included for selected recipients when they are part of the plan.

Why it matters

Where this can actually be useful.

Personal continuity

Useful if private instructions, context, or sensitive information should only be shared when you genuinely stop responding.

Family or trusted contacts

Useful if chosen people may need guidance, documents, or practical details that you would normally provide yourself.

Small operational fallback

Useful if a simple private handover should reach the right people when regular check-ins stop.

Privacy-first self-hosting

Useful if you want the whole process to stay on systems you already trust and control.

How it works

What happens in practice.

  1. You receive a check-in email

    totmann sends you a regular email asking you to confirm that you are still there.

  2. You confirm and the routine continues

    As long as you confirm in time, the cycle resets and the prepared handover stays untouched.

  3. A meaningful gap changes the state

    If confirmations stop for long enough, totmann treats that as a real absence rather than a single missed moment.

  4. The prepared handover goes out

    The prepared recipient messages are sent individually, and extra follow-up can stop once receipt has been confirmed.

Summary

What totmann actually does for you.

Quiet in normal life

When your regular routine is working, totmann stays in the background instead of demanding constant attention.

Prepared before it is needed

The timing, recipient list, messages, and optional files are all decided in advance, before there is any urgency.

Problems do not stay hidden

If totmann detects a setup or runtime problem, it can send a separate warning mail to your own address so you hear about it directly.

Protected links, clear trail

Detailed logs help you review what happened, and the action links are protected with signed tokens, including HMAC-based signing.

Personal rather than broadcast

Escalation messages go out one by one, which keeps the handover clearer and more private than a shared blast.

Clear stop to follow-up

Once receipt has been confirmed for an event, the additional follow-up can end cleanly.
